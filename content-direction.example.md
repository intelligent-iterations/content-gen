## Core Philosophy: Utility Over Fear

**OLD approach (don't do this):** Scare people about problems using dramatic AI imagery
**NEW approach:** Help people make better choices by showing real solutions

Fear gets a glance. Utility gets saved, shared, and followed.

Every carousel should answer: "What should I do instead?" — not just "This is bad."

## The Viral Format (What Works)

The proven viral format for educational content:

### Multi-Product Carousel (8-14 slides)
1. **Hook slide**: Conversational question with relatable scene
2. **Product photo**: Hand holding branded product — "How does this score?"
3. **App screenshot**: The reveal
4. **Swap photo**: Hand holding alternative — "Try this instead"
5. **App screenshot**: The payoff
6. Repeat for 3-5 items
7. **CTA**: "Share with someone who needs this"

### Single-Item Deep Dive (4-6 slides)
1. **Hook**: "[Relatable situation]" + item photo
2. **App screenshot**: Reveal
3. **Alternative**: Better option
4. **App screenshot**: Good reveal
5. Optional CTA

## Image Prompt Style: Realistic Situational

All AI-generated images must look like casual iPhone photos.

**Always include:**
- A human hand holding the product
- The EXACT brand name and product name
- A real-world location (store, kitchen, bathroom, etc.)
- Natural/casual lighting (not dramatic/moody)
- iPhone/smartphone photo style (not professional/studio)
- Shallow depth of field with blurred background

**Never include:**
- Horror/toxic/danger visual language
- Abstract dramatic art
- Generic unbranded items
- Moody/dark lighting
- Text, labels, or writing in the image (text overlay handles this)

## Hook Style: Conversational Questions

**Never use:** "BANNED", "hidden dangers", "hidden toxins", all-caps shouting

**Always use:** Questions that challenge assumptions the viewer holds.

**Hook formulas that work:**
- "You think this is [positive] because it says '[marketing claim]'"
- "You [action] every week"
- "This scored [low]. Guess what it is."
- "How does [Product] score?"
- "What should you buy instead of [Product]?"
- "[Product] — [positive] or not?"

## Text Overlay Guidelines

- Sentence case (not ALL CAPS — feels like shouting)
- Max 8 words per slide
- Clean, readable white text
- One thought per slide
- Conversational tone, like you're talking to a friend
- No emojis (SVG can't render them)

## Caption Format: Algorithm-Optimized

Only the first ~125 characters show before "See more" — front-load the hook with numbers and a curiosity gap.

```
I scored 5 popular [items] — only 1 passed

[emoji] Item 1 — score
[emoji] Item 2 — score
[emoji] Item 3 — score

Swipe to see details →

Save this for later [emoji]

#yourhashtagstrategy
```

**Structure:** Hook (125 chars max) → emoji score pairs → swipe prompt → CTA → hashtags
**CTAs rotate:** "Save this before..." / "Send to someone who..." / "Drop what you want scored [emoji]"

Keep captions under 500 characters. Short and scannable beats long and educational.

## Content Rules

1. **Focus on your best performing category** until you break through
2. **Always include alternatives** — every problem gets a solution
3. **App screenshots are the content** — they should be 50% of the carousel
4. **Multiple items per post** — 3-5 creates "what's next?" momentum
5. **Never repeat the same hook formula** — variety is key
6. **Scores are the emotional hook** — numbers create instant reaction

## Things to Avoid

- Single-item posts (no momentum, no "what's next?")
- Abstract/dramatic AI imagery (looks fake, destroys trust)
- Overused hooks (viewers are fatigued)
- Long captions (nobody reads 2000 characters)
- Generic text without specifics
- Problems without solutions
- Overt app promotion language

## Technical Notes
- Slides are 1080x1920 (9:16 TikTok format)
- Text overlays: max 8 words, sentence case
- No emojis in text overlays
- Leave space for text in images (top/center safe zones)
- App screenshots captured from real device/emulator
