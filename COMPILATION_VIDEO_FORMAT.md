# Compilation Video Format

How to stitch multiple ingredient character clips into a single viral video.

---

## The Numbers

From the viral videos that work:

| Format | Total Length | Clips | Per Clip | Ingredients |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Short (TikTok sweet spot)** | 30-45s | 5-6 | 6s each | 5-6 |
| **Medium (most viral)** | 45-60s | 6-8 | 6-8s each | 6-8 |
| **Long (max before drop-off)** | 60-70s | 8-10 | 6-7s each | 8-10 |

**Default: 6 clips x 6 seconds = 36s raw → ~40-45s with transitions.** This is the sweet spot.

---

## The Structure

Every compilation video follows this skeleton:

```
[CLIP 1] HOOK CHARACTER -- the most visually dramatic ingredient (0-6s)
[CLIP 2] SECOND CHARACTER -- contrasting color/energy from clip 1 (6-12s)
[CLIP 3] THIRD CHARACTER (12-18s)
[CLIP 4] FOURTH CHARACTER (18-24s)
[CLIP 5] FIFTH CHARACTER (24-30s)
[CLIP 6] CLOSER CHARACTER -- the most recognizable/popular ingredient (30-36s)
[END CARD] Optional: 2-3s branded outro or loop back to clip 1
```

### Clip 1: The Hook (Most Important)

This clip decides if someone keeps scrolling or watches. Rules:

- **Most visually dramatic character and action** -- not the most popular ingredient, the most VISUALLY ARRESTING one
- Best candidates: Glycolic acid punching through skin, Salicylic acid diving into a pore, Retinol ripping off dead skin
- For villain compilations: the most shocking/disturbing visual first
- **No intro title card, no "Part 1 of..." text, no preamble** -- character is mid-action frame 1
- The first frame must make sense as a **thumbnail** because TikTok shows it as the preview

### Clips 2-5: The Middle

- **Alternate energy levels** -- don't put two calm hydrators back to back. Alternate aggressive/gentle, bright/dark, warm/cool
- **Alternate colors** -- if clip 1 is green (glycolic), clip 2 should be blue (HA) or gold (retinol), not another green
- **Each clip is self-contained** -- viewers who skip ahead should still understand any individual clip
- **Maintain consistent skin macro setting** across all clips so it feels like one video, not six random ones

### Clip 6: The Closer

- **Most recognizable/popular ingredient** -- save the one everyone knows for last (Vitamin C, Retinol, Hyaluronic Acid)
- This is the "reward" for watching to the end
- OR for villain compilations: the most serious/impactful warning last
- Should feel like a natural conclusion, not an abrupt stop

### End Card (Optional)

- 2-3 seconds max
- "Follow for Part 2" or "Save this" CTA
- OR: loop seamlessly back to clip 1 for TikTok auto-loop (the first and last frames should feel connected)

## Ordering Strategy

### By Energy Arc (Recommended for Hero Videos)

```
AGGRESSIVE → GENTLE → AGGRESSIVE → GENTLE → AGGRESSIVE → CROWD FAVORITE
```

Example:
1. Glycolic Acid (punches through dead skin -- HIGH energy)
2. Hyaluronic Acid (dives into skin, plumps -- CALM energy)
3. Salicylic Acid (blasts debris from pore -- HIGH energy)
4. Niacinamide (builds shield wall -- CALM energy)
5. Retinol (rips off dead skin -- HIGH energy)
6. Vitamin C (radiates golden light -- SATISFYING closer)

### By Routine Order (Educational Videos)

```
CLEANSER INGREDIENT → EXFOLIANT → TREATMENT → SERUM → MOISTURIZER → SPF
```

Example:
1. Salicylic Acid (cleanser/exfoliant)
2. Glycolic Acid (exfoliant)
3. Niacinamide (treatment)
4. Vitamin C (serum)
5. Hyaluronic Acid (hydration)
6. SPF (protection -- always last in routine)

### By Escalation (Villain/Warning Videos)

```
MILD MISTAKE → WORSE MISTAKE → WORST MISTAKE → CONSEQUENCE → CONSEQUENCE → BIGGEST CONSEQUENCE
```

Example:
1. Skipping moisturizer (mild)
2. Over-exfoliating (worse)
3. Mixing acids (dangerous)
4. Sleeping in makeup (gross)
5. No SPF (serious damage)
6. Popping pimples (scarring -- visceral closer)

## Transitions Between Clips

### What Works (From Viral Analysis)
- **Hard cut** -- the most common. Clip ends, next clip starts. No fancy transition. TikTok viewers are used to this.
- **Quick dissolve** -- 3-5 frame crossfade. Slightly smoother than hard cut.
- **Match cut on skin** -- end clip on a skin close-up, start next clip on skin close-up with new character already there. Feels seamless.

### What Doesn't Work
- Elaborate transitions (star wipes, spirals, etc.) -- looks amateur
- Black frames between clips -- kills momentum, viewer scrolls away
- Title cards between clips ("Next: Vitamin C!") -- wastes time, breaks flow
- Slow fades -- too slow for short-form content

### Transition Rule
**The end of clip N and the start of clip N+1 should both show SKIN.** This creates visual continuity even with hard cuts. Each character appears on a skin landscape, so cutting between skin surfaces feels natural.

---

## Audio Strategy

### Background Music
- **ONE continuous track** across the entire compilation -- do NOT use separate music per clip
- Upbeat, energetic, royalty-free beauty/lifestyle music
- The music provides continuity when visuals jump between characters
- Add the music track in post after stitching all clips

### Voice
- Each clip has its own character voice (generated by Grok natively)
- Voices should naturally contrast between clips (the variety of characters handles this)
- Brief silence (0.2-0.5s) between clips is fine -- creates natural rhythm

### Sound Effects
- Keep whatever ambient SFX Grok generates per clip
- Don't add additional SFX in post unless something feels empty

## Thematic Groupings (Video Ideas)

## Video Categories

Every video falls into one of these four formats:

### Format A: "This Is What I Am" (Hero Explainer)
The ingredient is the hero. It introduces itself, shows what it does, explains why it's good for you.
- **Mood:** Wonder
- **Structure:** Character appears → does its function → tells you the benefit
- **Example:** "I'm Glycerin. I lock in moisture and keep your skin soft, plump, and glowing."

### Format B: "This Is What I Am" (Villain Explainer)
The ingredient/substance is the villain. It introduces itself, shows what it does to you, reveals why it's bad.
- **Mood:** Fear
- **Structure:** Character appears → does its damage → tells you the consequence
- **Example:** "I'm Nitrite. I make your meat look fresh... while your cells pay the price."

### Format C: "Good Thing Used Wrong" (Twist)
A normally good product or ingredient becomes harmful because of HOW you use it, WHAT it's paired with, or WHAT'S hidden inside it.
- **Mood:** Fear (starts wonder, twists to fear)
- **Structure:** Character appears looking friendly → does something wrong → consequence reveals the danger
- **Beat 1:** The product/ingredient looks helpful, trustworthy
- **Beat 2:** The twist -- the bad usage, the hidden ingredient, the wrong context
- **Beat 3:** The consequence plays out, character reveals the truth
- **Examples:**
  - Retinol character happily applying itself, then the SUN hits and the skin burns and peels. "I'm Retinol. Use me in the sun and I'll destroy what I'm supposed to protect."
  - Peanut butter jar character looking wholesome, then splits open to reveal hydrogenated oil oozing out. "I'm your peanut butter. But my secret ingredient is fully hydrogenated oil."
  - Sodium benzoate and Vitamin C characters high-five, then FUSE into a toxic molecule. "We're harmless alone. Together, we make a carcinogen."
  - Plastic teabag character sitting in hot water, tiny plastic particles breaking off and floating. "I'm your teabag. At 95 degrees, I release billions of microplastics into your cup."

### Format D: "Hidden Danger" (Exposure)
The everyday object or product is the focus. You think it's safe, but something about it is quietly harming you.
- **Mood:** Fear
- **Structure:** Normal-looking object → zoom into what's actually happening at micro level → reveal the harm
- **Examples:**
  - Non-stick pan character looking clean and shiny, then at macro level the coating is flaking off into food. "I'm your non-stick pan. My coating breaks down into forever chemicals in your body."
  - Scented candle character glowing warmly, then the smoke particles zoom in to show them entering lungs. "I smell amazing. But my fragrance is 30 chemicals you're breathing in."

## Post-Production Assembly Checklist

After generating all individual clips:

- [ ] All clips are the same aspect ratio (9:16)
- [ ] Order clips by the chosen strategy (energy arc, routine order, or escalation)
- [ ] Trim each clip to remove dead frames at head/tail (target 5-7s per clip)
- [ ] Hard cut or quick dissolve between each clip
- [ ] Add ONE continuous background music track across the full video
- [ ] Total runtime is between 30-60 seconds
- [ ] First frame works as a thumbnail (visually arresting, character visible)
- [ ] Last clip feels like a natural conclusion, not an abrupt stop
- [ ] No black frames, title cards, or dead space between clips
- [ ] Audio levels are consistent across clips (normalize)
- [ ] Export at 9:16, minimum 720p, 24fps

## FFmpeg Stitch Command (Quick Reference)

```bash
# Create a file list
echo "file 'clip1.mp4'" > list.txt
echo "file 'clip2.mp4'" >> list.txt
echo "file 'clip3.mp4'" >> list.txt
echo "file 'clip4.mp4'" >> list.txt
echo "file 'clip5.mp4'" >> list.txt
echo "file 'clip6.mp4'" >> list.txt

# Concatenate clips
ffmpeg -f concat -safe 0 -i list.txt -c copy stitched.mp4

# Add background music (lower music volume to -15dB so voice is clear)
ffmpeg -i stitched.mp4 -i bgm.mp3 -filter_complex "[1:a]volume=0.15[bgm];[0:a][bgm]amix=inputs=2:duration=first[a]" -map 0:v -map "[a]" -shortest final_video.mp4
```
